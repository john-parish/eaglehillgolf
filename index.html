About Eagle Hills

A hometown course with big-league golf inside.

Eagle Hills sits just outside town on Country Club Road, where nine holes wind through the kind of open, rolling ground that only north-central Iowa can grow. It's the course where Algona learned to golf — and where it still comes to play.

A few years back, we brought golf indoors too. Four Full Swing simulator bays mean a Tuesday-night lesson or a January round with the guys doesn't have to wait for May. Add a scratch kitchen turning out butter burgers, wood-fired pizza, and steak dinners, and Eagle Hills has quietly become less of a golf course and more of a gathering place.

We're not trying to be River Road, or Des Moines, or anywhere but here. We're trying to be the best version of a small-town Iowa golf club — and thirteen honest Google reviews at 4.5 stars say we're getting there.

FAQ

Questions before you tee off.

Do I need golf experience to book a simulator bay?

Not at all. Our indoor bays welcome everyone from first-timers to scratch golfers. Staff can help you get set up, and clubs are available if you don't have your own.

What's included with an Eagle Hills membership?

Membership includes unlimited access to the 9-hole course for the season. Add a cart shed spot to store your cart on-site, or choose the student rate if you qualify. Simulator time and Grille purchases are billed separately.

Can I rent clubs, or do I need my own?

Both options work. Bring your own set for the course or the simulator bays, or ask our pro shop staff about rental clubs when you arrive.

Do you host leagues, corporate outings, or fundraisers?

Yes. Eagle Hills regularly hosts men's and women's leagues, company scrambles, church and charity outings, and clubhouse gatherings. Call ahead to reserve a date and we'll help you plan it.

Is the Grille open to people who aren't golfing?

Absolutely — the Grille & Bar is open to the whole community, whether you played nine holes or just stopped by for a butter burger and a beer.

What happens to my tee time if the weather turns?

Iowa weather does what it wants. If the course closes for rain or storms, call the pro shop and we'll get you rebooked — no hassle, no charge.
